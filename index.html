The cheetah is a large cat that is the fastest land animal. It can run at speeds of 50 to 61 mph. Cheetahs have evolved adaptations for speed, long thin legs, and a long tail. Their long tail helps with balance and changing direction quickly.

Cheetahs are native to Africa, central Iran, and India.

Why is that cheetah the fastest animal?

  • Lightweight body: Cheetahs weigh between 80 and 140 lbs. Their lightweight bodies help them gain speed faster.
  • Small head: Their small heads are designed for aerodynamics.
  • Long legs: Their long legs are designed for aerodynamics.
  • Flexible spine: Their flexible spine allows for extreme extension during the run.
  • Semi-retractable claws: Their semi-retractable claws act like running spikes.
